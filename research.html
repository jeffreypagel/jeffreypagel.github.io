Working Papers

More Than Just Carbon: The Socioeconomic Impact of Large-Scale Tree Planting
with Lorenzo Sileci and Charles Palmer
This paper studies the poverty effects of the Philippines’ National Greening Program, showing gains in tree cover alongside reductions in poverty and shifts in local labor markets.
Working Paper VoxDev
Mangrove Planting, Changing Fish Markets and Food Security
with Charles Palmer and Lorenzo Sileci
This project examines how mangrove restoration affects fisheries, prices, and household diets, finding more fishing activity but short-run pressure on productivity and food consumption patterns.
Aid Against Trees? Evidence from a Community-Driven Development Program in the Philippines
Using quasi-experimental evidence, this paper studies whether community-driven development programs can unintentionally increase deforestation, especially through infrastructure-related investments.
Paper
Multidimensional Energy Poverty: A Quasi-experimental Approach Applied to Education
This paper develops a multidimensional energy poverty index and applies it to rural Uganda to study how overlapping energy constraints shape educational outcomes.
